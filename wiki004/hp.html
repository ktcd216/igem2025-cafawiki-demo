Human Practices

Bridging synthetic biology with societal needs and ethical considerations

Our Human Practices work ensures that TimeEraser addresses real-world needs responsibly. Through stakeholder engagement, ethical analysis, and continuous iteration, we've shaped a project that is both scientifically innovative and socially conscious.

Social Needs & Public Engagement

Identifying Societal Needs

We conducted extensive research to understand the societal implications of aging interventions:

  • Interviewed 15 geriatric specialists about clinical needs in aging research
  • Surveyed 200+ community members on perceptions of lifespan extension
  • Analyzed policy documents on aging population challenges in China

"The most valuable aging research doesn't just extend life, but extends healthy, productive years. This team's focus on healthspan aligns perfectly with clinical priorities."

— Dr. Zhang Li, Gerontology Department, Beijing Hospital

Stakeholder Engagement

Key stakeholder interactions that shaped our project:

  • Roundtable discussion with bioethicists from the Chinese Academy of Sciences
  • Collaboration with longevity startup founders on commercialization pathways
  • Consultation with policymakers about regulatory frameworks for aging interventions

These conversations led us to focus on healthspan extension rather than just lifespan, addressing ethical concerns about overpopulation while meeting medical needs.

Ethics & Safety

Ethical Considerations

We addressed several ethical dimensions of our research:

  • Conducted risk-benefit analysis of cellular rejuvenation therapies
  • Evaluated equity of access issues with potential future treatments
  • Developed guidelines for responsible communication of aging research

Our ethical framework was reviewed by the university's Institutional Review Board and incorporated into all public communications.

Biosafety Implementation

Safety measures integrated throughout our project:

  • All engineered strains contain dual kill switches for biocontainment
  • Regular safety training sessions for all team members
  • Consultation with iGEM Safety Committee for risk assessment

We implemented additional containment measures after community members expressed concerns about engineered organisms in aging research.

Policy & Education

Policy Research

Our analysis of current regulations and policy recommendations:

  • Mapped regulatory pathways for cellular rejuvenation therapies in China, US, and EU
  • Identified gaps in oversight for gene therapies targeting aging
  • Proposed framework for staged clinical testing of healthspan interventions

We presented our findings to the National Health Commission's emerging technologies working group.

Education & Outreach

Our initiatives to engage diverse audiences:

  • Developed aging biology curriculum for high school students (reached 500+ students)
  • Created bilingual explainer videos about synthetic biology and aging research
  • Organized community science fair with hands-on demos

All materials were tested with focus groups to ensure accessibility.

Project Evolution Through Human Practices

Initial Concept

Focused on lifespan extension through telomerase activation

Stakeholder Feedback

Pivoted to healthspan after medical professionals emphasized quality of life

Safety Enhancements

Added redundant biocontainment after community consultations

Final Design

Integrated biomarkers for healthspan assessment based on clinical input
