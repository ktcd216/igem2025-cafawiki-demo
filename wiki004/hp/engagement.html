Our Engagement Philosophy

Beyond Consultation: Co-Creation

We rejected the traditional one-way consultation model in favor of a dynamic co-creation process with stakeholders. This approach ensured our synthetic biology solution was not just scientifically sound, but also socially responsible and practically implementable.

"The most valuable insights came from unexpected places - home caregivers revealed usability issues our lab team would never have anticipated."

— Team Member Reflection

The Engagement Timeline

1

Stakeholder Mapping (March)

Identified 7 key stakeholder groups through:

  • Ecosystem analysis workshops
  • Expert interviews with bioethicists
  • Review of similar case studies
2

Deep-Dive Interviews (April-May)

Conducted 23 semi-structured interviews across:

Clinicians (6) Patients (8) Industry (5) Regulators (4)

Key question: "What would make this technology truly useful in your context?"

3

Co-Design Workshops (June)

Facilitated 3 interactive sessions with mixed stakeholder groups to:

  • Prioritize features through dot voting
  • Create journey maps for different user types
  • Prototype alternative solutions

Reflections & Lessons

What We Learned

Unexpected Insights

Home caregivers identified more usability issues than clinicians, highlighting the value of engaging end-user ecosystems.

Power Dynamics

We learned to actively mitigate power imbalances in discussions between patients and medical professionals.

Iterative Process

Meaningful engagement requires multiple touchpoints, not one-off consultations.
