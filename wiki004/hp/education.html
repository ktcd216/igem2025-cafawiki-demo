  • Demystify synthetic biology and aging research for diverse audiences
  • Inspire STEM interest among K-12 students through hands-on activities
  • Address public questions and concerns about genetic engineering
  • Create accessible resources that outlast our iGEM project

School Workshops & Science Fairs

We designed interactive synthetic biology workshops for students aged 12-18, adapting content for different education levels:

  • DNA Extraction Lab: Students extracted DNA from strawberries using household materials
  • Aging Biology Demo: Interactive station comparing young vs. senescent cells
  • Career Panels: Discussions with researchers about paths in biotechnology

Impact & Future Directions

Our education efforts directly influenced both our community and our project:

  • Student questions led us to simplify our public explanations of cellular aging
  • Teacher feedback helped us identify common misconceptions about genetic engineering
  • Community concerns prompted us to enhance our safety documentation

We're committed to sustaining these initiatives through:

  • Ongoing partnership with local science centers
  • Annual synthetic biology summer camp for high school students
  • Open-access repository of all educational materials
