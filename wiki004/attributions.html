Attributions & Acknowledgments

Our TimeEraser project is the result of collective effort, guidance, and support. We extend our sincere gratitude to everyone who contributed to our iGEM journey.

Meet the Contributors

Our Team's Contributions

Each member of the CAFA-AFAC iGEM team played a vital role in bringing the TimeEraser project to life. Here's a breakdown of our individual contributions.

Alice Chen

Project Lead & Wet Lab

Alice conceptualized the core idea of TimeEraser and spearheaded the genetic circuit design. She was instrumental in all molecular cloning experiments, plasmid assembly, and bacterial transformations. Her leadership kept the team focused and motivated.

Bob Singh

Modeling & Bioinformatics

Bob developed all computational models for TimeEraser, including kinetic simulations of our genetic circuit and predictive analyses of protein interactions. He also managed all bioinformatics data, sequence analysis, and contributed significantly to the wiki design's interactive elements.

Carol Lee

Human Practices & Outreach

Carol was the driving force behind our Human Practices initiatives. She organized public surveys, conducted interviews with experts in aging research and bioethics, and led our educational workshops in local schools. Her dedication ensured our project's societal relevance.

David Kim

Safety & Lab Management

David meticulously ensured all lab operations adhered to safety protocols. He managed equipment, prepared reagents, and maintained our strain collection. He also compiled our comprehensive safety report and developed the emergency procedures flowchart.

Mentors, Advisors & Collaborators

Dr. Sophia Reyes - Principal Investigator

Provided invaluable scientific guidance, strategic project direction, and ensured all research adhered to ethical guidelines. Her expertise in synthetic biology was critical.

Professor Ben Carter - Academic Advisor

Offered profound insights into molecular genetics and helped troubleshoot complex experimental challenges. His mentorship was crucial for our lab work.

Ms. Emily Wong - Lab Technician

Assisted tirelessly with lab setup, reagent preparation, and training new team members on core protocols. Her organizational skills were essential.

Data Science Department, University X - Collaborators

Provided access to high-performance computing resources and statistical analysis tools that significantly enhanced our modeling efforts.

Our Generous Sponsors

We are incredibly grateful for the financial and in-kind support from our sponsors, without whom the TimeEraser project would not have been possible.

General Support & Community

Our sincere thanks go out to the entire iGEM community for their inspiration and support throughout the competition. We've learned immensely from other teams and the collaborative spirit of synthetic biology.

Special thanks also to our families and friends for their unwavering encouragement and patience during long lab hours.

Materials & Software Attributions

We acknowledge the following companies and developers for providing essential materials, software, and resources:

  • **IDT (Integrated DNA Technologies):** For providing custom DNA oligonucleotides and gene synthesis services.
  • **New England Biolabs (NEB):** For supplying restriction enzymes, ligases, and other molecular biology reagents.
  • **SnapGene:** For their intuitive software, which was indispensable for plasmid design and visualization.
  • **Python & Jupyter Notebooks:** Used extensively for data analysis, modeling, and visualization.
  • **Open Science Framework (OSF):** For hosting our project data and documentation.
