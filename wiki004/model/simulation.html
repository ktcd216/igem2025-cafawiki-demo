Modeling: Simulation

Simulating peptide delivery and system behavior under varied conditions using mathematical and computational tools.

View Simulation Workflow
Purpose Environment Assumptions Scenarios Results Validation Implication

Purpose of Simulation

The simulation helps evaluate our peptide delivery model before in vivo testing, offering predictive insight on diffusion and efficiency.

Simulation Environment

We used MATLAB and Python for solving PDEs and visualizing peptide distribution across skin layers over time.

Assumptions

We assumed homogeneous skin thickness, isotropic diffusion, and stable environmental temperature across simulation runs.

Simulated Scenarios

Three conditions were tested: ① baseline diffusion, ② enhanced with microneedle, ③ suppressed permeability to mimic aged skin.

Results

The diffusion profile showed significant enhancement under microneedle treatment, with maximum delivery achieved within 4~6 hours.

Validation

Simulation outputs were compared to in vitro test data, showing high consistency in trend and final concentration levels.

Implication

Simulation supports the feasibility of our peptide design and guides formulation timing, microneedle specs, and expected dose-response.
