Data Analysis

We processed, analyzed, and interpreted experimental and simulated data to validate our model and extract biological insights.

Overview

This section details how our raw data from wet lab experiments and simulations were transformed into validated conclusions. We followed a structured workflow covering collection, cleaning, visualization, statistical analysis, and biological interpretation.

Data Sources

We collected data from:

  • Protein expression levels from SDS-PAGE and fluorescence imaging
  • Absorption rates from microneedle experiments
  • Simulated output from our MATLAB model (CSV exports)

Preprocessing Methods

Data was processed using Python (Pandas, NumPy) to handle missing values, normalize intensity readings, and align time points across batches. Outlier detection was performed using the IQR method, and smoothing was applied to reduce measurement noise.

Statistical Analysis

Statistical tests were applied to determine significance of observed effects:

  • T-tests for comparing expression under different conditions
  • ANOVA for multi-factor peptide delivery experiments
  • Linear regression to correlate delivery rate with peptide mass

Data Interpretation

Data showed that collagen peptides fused with CPPs demonstrated significantly higher intracellular uptake. Results supported our hypothesis that fusion design and delivery method impact biological performance, aligning with our predictive models.

Limitations & Improvements

While our data supports the model, limitations include small sample sizes, variability in biological replicates, and simplified assumptions in simulation. Future work will involve time-course analysis, expanded datasets, and Bayesian statistical integration.
