Our Project: TimeEraser Overview

Delve into the core of CAFA-AFAC's TimeEraser project, from its conceptualization to its potential impact on biology and society.

Project Vision

Defining the TimeEraser Mission

Our Goal: Extending Healthy Lifespan

The TimeEraser project is fundamentally about understanding and manipulating the biological processes of aging. Our primary goal is to engineer a system that can not only slow down but potentially reverse specific markers of cellular senescence, contributing to extended healthy lifespans.

This mission goes beyond simple longevity; it's about enhancing the quality of life in later years by addressing the root causes of age-related decline.

Innovation Through Synthetic Biology

We apply principles of synthetic biology to design custom genetic circuits capable of precise intervention. This involves engineering cells to produce specific enzymes or regulatory molecules that can 'reset' or modulate aging pathways, offering a novel approach to an ancient problem.

Our work is highly interdisciplinary, combining molecular biology, bioinformatics, and advanced engineering principles to create a robust and predictable biological system.

TimeEraser's Core Methodology

Bioinformatics Driven Design

Leveraging computational tools to identify key genetic targets and predict circuit behavior.

Wet Lab Experimentation

Rigorous in-vitro and ex-vivo experiments validating our engineered constructs.

Modular Genetic Circuits

Designing plug-and-play biological modules for scalability and adaptability.

Iterative Optimization

Continuous refinement of our system based on experimental feedback and modeling.

Expected Outcomes & Societal Impact

Potential for Healthy Longevity

Our project aims to provide foundational insights and potentially a proof-of-concept for therapies that could extend the period of healthy, active living. This could alleviate burdens on healthcare systems and improve individual well-being.

Broader Applications in Biotechnology

Beyond aging, the precision engineering techniques and modular biological circuits developed in TimeEraser have implications for various biotechnological applications, from disease modeling to drug discovery and advanced biomanufacturing.

Challenges & Future Directions

During the TimeEraser project, we faced significant challenges in achieving precise gene expression control and ensuring the stability of our engineered constructs. Through rigorous debugging, iterative design, and leveraging advanced bioinformatics, we successfully overcame these hurdles.

Our future work includes exploring in-vivo applications, optimizing the system for different cell types, and further investigating the long-term effects and potential off-target interactions. We are also keen on collaborating with other research groups to expand the scope of TimeEraser.

We plan to broaden our human practices initiatives, engaging with a wider public audience and policy makers to foster informed discussions about the future of synthetic biology and its implications for human health and society. Ethical guidance will continue to shape our research direction.
