Our Commitment to Safety

At CAFA-AFAC iGEM, safety is paramount. We adhere to the highest standards to ensure responsible and ethical synthetic biology practices in our TimeEraser project.

View Risk Assessment

General Lab Safety

Our team operates in a Biosafety Level 1 (BSL-1) laboratory, compliant with all institutional and national regulations. All team members have undergone comprehensive safety training and are proficient in sterile techniques and waste disposal.

Our BSL-1 Environment

The BSL-1 laboratory is designed for work with well-characterized agents not known to consistently cause disease in healthy adult humans, and of minimal potential hazard to laboratory personnel and the environment. This includes standard microbiological practices.

  • No open-toed shoes or shorts.
  • Lab coats and safety glasses mandatory.
  • Strict handwashing protocols.
  • Controlled access and clear signage.

Spill Response Protocol

For chemical or biological spills, immediate action is crucial. Ensure appropriate personal protective equipment (PPE) is worn, contain the spill, and use designated spill kits. Report all incidents to the lab supervisor immediately.

Responsible Conduct & Dual Use Concerns

We are acutely aware of the potential dual-use implications of synthetic biology research. Our project, TimeEraser, is developed with the sole intention of advancing scientific understanding and benefiting humanity. We regularly review our work to prevent misuse and maintain transparency.

All team members are educated on the ethical responsibilities associated with their research and the importance of open communication regarding potential risks or concerns.

Safety FAQ

Is E. coli DH5Î± safe to work with?

Yes, E. coli DH5Î± is a widely used and well-characterized laboratory strain that is considered non-pathogenic to healthy humans. It's a Biosafety Level 1 (BSL-1) organism, meaning it poses minimal risk.

What should I do if a biological spill occurs?

Immediately alert others in the vicinity. Cover the spill with absorbent material (e.g., paper towels), then apply disinfectant (e.g., 10% bleach solution) for at least 20 minutes. Dispose of all materials in biohazard waste. Inform your supervisor.

How is our biological waste disposed of?

All biological waste, including culture media, contaminated plastics, and bacterial cultures, is collected in designated biohazard bags and autoclaved at appropriate temperatures and pressures to ensure complete sterilization before disposal.

Are our engineered constructs safe for the environment?

Our TimeEraser constructs are designed for contained laboratory use and are not intended for environmental release. We use non-integrating plasmids and host strains with attenuated survival outside the lab, minimizing any potential environmental impact.
