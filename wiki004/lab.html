Recombinant Collagen Peptide Lab

Explore the design and testing behind our multifunctional collagen peptide, including its synthesis, delivery, and cost-effective production methods.

Overview

We are developing a recombinant collagen peptide with whitening, anti-aging, and transdermal absorption capabilities, addressing current issues such as high cost, poor absorption, and unclear efficacy in existing collagen products.

Collagen Peptide Design

The target sequence includes three core functional domains: a melanin-inhibiting peptide mimicking α-MSH, a growth factor-like fragment that promotes collagen synthesis, and a CPP cell-penetrating peptide to enhance skin permeability — achieving multi-functionality in one.

Expression Strategy

We utilize an E. coli N1917 expression system to perform multifunctional fusion expression using a single plasmid, enabling efficient protein expression and simplified culture processes to improve feasibility and control costs.

Transdermal Delivery

Microneedle carriers are used as the delivery platform to help peptides penetrate the stratum corneum, achieving effective transdermal absorption and solving the common issue of poor absorption in topical skincare products.

Cost Optimization

Fusion expression avoids segmented cultivation and purification. A unified expression system enhances yield and significantly reduces production costs, boosting the market potential of collagen peptides.
