Overview

Our model aims to quantitatively describe the interaction between our recombinant collagen peptide system and the target biological pathways. It serves to simulate protein expression kinetics, peptide diffusion behavior, and the predicted efficacy under various delivery scenarios.

Mathematical Assumptions

To simplify our system, the following assumptions were made:

  • Protein production follows Michaelis-Menten kinetics.
  • Peptide diffusion through the skin is one-dimensional.
  • Cell penetration efficiency is constant per CPP unit.
  • Degradation is modeled via exponential decay.

Model Construction

We formulated a system of differential equations to describe the kinetics of transcription, translation, degradation, and transport. Ordinary differential equations (ODEs) were used to capture changes in protein concentration over time in both intracellular and extracellular environments.

Simulation & Results

Numerical simulation was carried out using MATLAB. The system converged within 120 minutes under assumed initial conditions. Our simulations showed that peptide delivery is most efficient when combined with sustained promoter activation and optimized plasmid dosage.

Sensitivity Analysis

We analyzed how output variables (e.g., peptide concentration) change in response to fluctuations in parameters such as promoter strength, degradation rate, and diffusion coefficient. This allows us to identify critical design levers that ensure robustness of our system.

Experimental Validation

Our model predictions were validated through wet-lab fluorescence data. Measured peptide levels in vitro were consistent with simulated outcomes, confirming the accuracy of the degradation and transdermal transport modules within the model.

Predictive Application

We extended the model to predict in-vivo outcomes for future applications. These predictions guide us in optimizing carrier materials, timing of delivery, and peptide structure to maximize therapeutic outcomes in future stages of the project.
