Overview

This lab focuses on developing a novel collagen peptide that combines whitening, anti-aging, and transdermal delivery functions. Our work explores cost-effective production and effective expression systems for these bioactive peptides.

Experimental Method

We designed a fusion protein comprising Œ±-MSH mimic, collagen-inducing domain, and CPP. The expression vector was constructed in E. coli and cultured under IPTG induction. Purification was performed using affinity chromatography.

Experiment Results

SDS-PAGE confirmed correct expression of the fusion protein. Western blot verified the presence of CPP and collagen domains. Cell assays demonstrated enhanced collagen production and reduced melanin levels.

Data Analysis

Quantitative image analysis showed 20% higher collagen levels in treated samples. The whitening effect was validated using melanin content quantification, revealing a 15% reduction compared to control.

Limitations & Notes

Challenges include incomplete solubility of fusion proteins and low transdermal absorption in standard formulations. Future optimizations include improving delivery carriers and enhancing expression stability.
